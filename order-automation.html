// overview

This system was built to remove manual order processing entirely. The moment an order email arrives, AI extraction reads the message the way a human would, matches each item against a pricing database, and calculates the order subtotal and total — no fixed format required from the customer. A unique Order ID is generated for every order, and a duplicate-order filter stops the same email from being processed twice before a confirmation with upsell suggestions is sent automatically.

Every order is logged into Google Sheets with its Order ID, customer name, order details, date, cost, and total payment, alongside a calculated profit figure that continuously updates an accumulated profit total. Three hours after an order is logged, the system reaches back out for feedback through a Tally form — collecting a 1–5 star rating and optional comments — with duplicate submissions filtered out before the verified response is logged. The rating then drives an intelligent, tiered reply, and a personalized reorder invitation goes out 24 hours later, with its tone shaped by how the customer originally rated the experience.

// impact

This system replaces what used to be manual reading, calculating, logging, and following up on every single order. Now, the moment an order email arrives, it's parsed by AI, priced against the catalog, assigned a unique Order ID, checked for duplicates, logged into Google Sheets with full cost and profit detail, and confirmed by email — all without a manual touch. The accumulated profit figure updates automatically with every order, giving a live read on the business's bottom line.

On the customer side, a 3-hour delayed Tally survey collects a star rating and comments, duplicate submissions are filtered out, and the verified feedback drives a tiered, rating-matched reply — from a thank-you at five stars to a recovery message at one star. A personalized reorder invitation follows 24 hours later, its tone shaped by the original rating, turning a single order into the start of a retention cycle.

The same pattern — detect → extract → process → confirm → collect feedback → respond → retain — can be adapted for service bookings, lead intake, or any business that depends on repeat customers.
